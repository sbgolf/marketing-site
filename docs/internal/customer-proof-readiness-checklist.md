# Customer proof readiness checklist

Internal governance checklist for collecting, approving, storing, and eventually publishing StartLine Sites customer proof. This checklist exists so future testimonials, logos, screenshots, before/after stories, and outcome claims are real, permissioned, and Steve-approved before they appear in public copy.

## Source of truth and intake model

- **Intake channel**: Email is the default intake channel for customer proof submissions, permission notes, wording approvals, and revocation requests.
- **Storage/source of truth**: Google Drive is the source of truth for proof asset files, supporting permission records, approved wording, and approval history.
- **Governance record**: This repo checklist is the governance standard. Do not treat the repo as proof-asset storage and do not commit real customer files, private customer notes, screenshots, logos, or testimonial source material here.
- **Approval requirement**: Steve approval is required before any real customer proof asset, customer name, logo, screenshot, quote, case study, or customer-specific metric is published.

## Google Drive folder model

Create a dedicated customer-proof Drive area with these top-level folders:

1. **Pending Review**
   - New or updated assets received by email.
   - Assets with incomplete permission details.
   - Draft wording awaiting customer and/or Steve approval.
2. **Approved**
   - Assets that have documented permission, approved wording, usage surfaces, reviewer, and approval date.
   - Assets ready for use only on the approved surfaces and only within the approved wording/usage limits.
3. **Blocked or Needs Permission**
   - Assets that cannot be used publicly yet.
   - Assets with unclear source, missing permission, expired approval, revoked permission, unsupported metrics, or unclear customer/customer-representative authority.

Optional later structure, once volume requires it:

- Per-race/customer subfolders under each status folder.
- Per-asset-type subfolders for testimonials, logos, screenshots, case studies, metrics, and emails.
- A Drive index sheet that mirrors the required metadata fields below.

## Asset statuses

Use one of these statuses for every proof asset:

- **Approved**: Permission, wording, usage surfaces, reviewer, and approval date are documented. Asset may be used only as approved.
- **Pending Review**: Asset has been received but is not ready for public use. It needs Steve review, customer permission, wording approval, metadata completion, or claim verification.
- **Blocked/Needs Permission**: Asset must not be published. Use this for missing/unclear permission, unsupported claims, expired/revoked permission, unverified metrics, or any asset Steve has not approved.

## Required per-asset metadata

Track this metadata in Drive, preferably in an index sheet and/or a metadata note stored with the asset:

- **Drive link**: Link to the source asset and any supporting approval thread/export.
- **Asset type**: Testimonial, logo, screenshot, before/after story, case study, metric, customer quote, email excerpt, sponsor proof, or other.
- **Source/person**: Customer/race name, submitting person, role/title if public, and whether that person has authority to grant permission.
- **Date received**: Date the asset, quote, or metric was received.
- **Permission status**: Approved, pending, blocked, expired, revoked, or needs clarification; include who granted permission and where the permission record lives.
- **Approved wording**: Exact approved public wording. Do not paraphrase quotes or outcome claims unless the revised wording is separately approved.
- **Approved usage surfaces**: Specific surfaces where the asset may appear, such as homepage, pricing page, sample audit page, proposal PDF, sales email, social post, case-study page, or internal-only.
- **Reviewer/approval date**: Steve or designated reviewer plus approval date.
- **Expiry/revocation notes**: Any end date, campaign limitation, customer opt-out, requested edits, removal obligations, or revocation history.

## Rules: never fabricate proof

Do **not** publish, imply, mock up, or style the following as real unless the asset is approved and tracked with complete metadata:

- Testimonials, quotes, endorsements, or “what customers say” copy.
- Customer logos or race logos.
- Live race screenshots, private mockup screenshots tied to a real customer, or before/after customer stories.
- Named case studies or named race examples.
- Customer-specific registration, traffic, conversion, revenue, speed, ranking, or sponsor metrics.
- Composite quotes that read like a real person said them.
- AI-generated screenshots, logos, or race/customer names presented as real.

If proof is fictional, sample, or generic, label it clearly. Keep examples respectful and avoid presenting fictional outcomes as evidence.

## Fallback proof alternatives when real assets are unavailable

When approved real customer proof is unavailable, use evidence-safe alternatives instead of inventing proof:

- **Fictional sample audits**: Clearly labeled fictional/generic audits showing how StartLine evaluates registration CTA clarity, mobile usability, logistics content, sponsor visibility, SEO basics, and race-week readiness.
- **Generic deliverable examples**: Non-customer-specific examples of checklists, page structures, launch plans, content outlines, or handoff notes.
- **Founder note**: A transparent note from Steve about the StartLine process, standards, and why the service exists.
- **BMQR background**: Factual BMQR background only when accurate, relevant, and not inflated into customer proof.
- **Transparent process/timeline**: Show how audits, private mockups, intake, build, review, launch, and post-launch handoff work.
- **Evidence-safe SEO/performance copy**: Explain clean structure, fast pages, metadata/schema where appropriate, content hierarchy, analytics readiness, and “Built for search, speed, and signups” without ranking or traffic guarantees.

## Self-audit/review checklist

Before moving any asset to **Approved** or publishing proof copy, confirm:

- [ ] The source asset lives in Google Drive, not in the repo.
- [ ] The asset has a clear status: Approved, Pending Review, or Blocked/Needs Permission.
- [ ] Permission is documented and tied to an authorized person/source.
- [ ] Exact approved wording is documented.
- [ ] Approved usage surfaces are documented.
- [ ] Steve or the designated reviewer and approval date are documented.
- [ ] Expiry, revocation, or usage limits are documented, if any.
- [ ] No quote, logo, screenshot, case study, customer name, or metric is fabricated.
- [ ] Metrics are supportable, sourced, and approved; no guaranteed-growth or ranking claims are introduced.
- [ ] Before/after framing is respectful and focuses on clarity, runner trust, registration confidence, race-director workload reduction, and sponsor value.
- [ ] Fictional or generic examples are labeled as fictional/generic and cannot be confused with real customer proof.
- [ ] Public copy does not expose private customer data, contact details, Stripe IDs, internal notes, or credentials.
- [ ] Steve approval is still required before merge or publication.
