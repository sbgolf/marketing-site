These notes summarize public examples and concepts used to inform the StartLine Sites services agreement draft. Do not copy external legal text verbatim. The draft should be reviewed before customer use.

## Concepts adapted, not copied

- Use plain-English responsibilities for both parties.
- Require 50% deposit before work begins and final balance at completion/launch.
- Define customer responsibilities for content, assets, approvals, domain/DNS, and legal rights.
- Separate included revisions from out-of-scope change requests.
- Treat hosting/maintenance as active only while the subscription is active.
- Start monthly billing at go-live and renew monthly until cancellation.
- Customer owns its domain, content, data, and final customer-specific content after payment.
- StartLine Sites retains templates, reusable components, tools, workflows, and know-how.
- Allow portfolio/case-study use unless the customer opts out.
- Disclaim guaranteed SEO, traffic, registrations, revenue, or race outcomes.
- Limit liability for indirect/consequential damages and cap direct claims where enforceable.
- Provide termination/cure/suspension paths for breach, nonpayment, unlawful content, and operational risk.

## Open legal placeholders

- Legal entity name and address
- Governing law
- Venue/arbitration language
- Cure period length: 10 vs 30 days
- Warranty/correction period length
- Liability cap wording and enforceability
- Whether terms acknowledgement should be required in Stripe checkout
