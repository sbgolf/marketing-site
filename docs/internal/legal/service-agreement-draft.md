## 1. What StartLine Sites does

StartLine Sites builds fast, SEO-optimized race websites and provides any approved race-cycle support described in the proposal to help runners find the race, trust the details, and click through to register on the customer's existing registration platform.

StartLine Sites does not replace RunSignup, Race Roster, Haku, Active, Let’s Do This, or any other registration platform. Registration, refunds, transfers, race operations, athlete communications, safety, and race-day execution remain the customer's responsibility.

## 3. Payment terms

### Deposit

A 50% setup deposit is due before production work begins.

- Starter deposit: $750
- Standard deposit: $1,250
- Premium deposit: $2,250

The deposit reserves the project and starts the kickoff process. StartLine Sites is not required to begin production work until the deposit is paid and confirmed.

### Final setup balance

The remaining 50% setup balance is invoiced through Stripe at launch and is due net 7.

- Starter final balance: $750
- Standard final balance: $1,250
- Premium final balance: $2,250

Default invoice wording:

```text
Final 50% balance for StartLine Sites [Starter / Standard / Premium] website setup for [Race Name]. Site launched at [Live URL] on [Launch Date]. Due net 7.
```

### Optional recurring service

No monthly plan starts by default. Any recurring service requires Steve approval and customer-specific written scope/billing confirmation before billing starts.

Approved recurring-service wording:

```text
Steve approved the StartLine Sites [Foundation / Growth / Performance / custom] recurring service for [Race Name]. Monthly billing is $[Amount]/month, starts on [Start Date], and renews each month until cancelled under the agreement.
```

Recurring fees are billed through Stripe only for approved recurring services and renew under the written approved scope until cancelled. Recurring fees already billed are non-refundable except where required by law or agreed in writing.

## 4. Customer responsibilities

The customer agrees to provide accurate and timely:

- Race name, date, location, distances, schedule, logistics, policies, and FAQs
- Registration URL and registration platform details
- Pricing, deadlines, caps, refund/transfer policies, and race-day details
- Logos, photos, sponsor logos with display rights/details, available course maps/route links/elevation details, and brand assets
- Domain/DNS access or a responsible person who can make DNS changes
- GA4/GSC/analytics access where needed
- Review feedback and approvals
- A primary decision-maker for approvals
- A valid payment method and current billing contact

The customer confirms it has the rights to use all content and assets it provides, including logos, photos, sponsor materials/logos, maps/route files, copy, trademarks, and race information.

Customer-owned delays extend the timeline. The delivery clock starts when deposit is paid and StartLine Sites has complete intake answers, usable assets, and required access.

## 5. StartLine Sites responsibilities

StartLine Sites agrees to:

- Build the selected setup package using StartLine Sites templates and standards
- Use commercially reasonable SEO, accessibility, performance, and conversion practices
- Connect registration CTAs to the customer's registration platform
- Set up core metadata, structured data, sitemap, and launch essentials included in the selected package
- Provide the included revision rounds
- Host/support the site as described in the approved first-year package scope
- Provide optional follow-up or recurring services only if separately approved in writing
- Respond to routine support requests within 2 business days

StartLine Sites may make technical changes needed for security, uptime, performance, accessibility, browser compatibility, or hosting reliability.

## 6. Scope, revisions, and changes

The included scope is the scope described in the approved proposal/order summary.

Included revisions cover reasonable text, image, link, and content corrections during the agreed review period.

The following are not included unless specifically listed in the proposal:

- Custom application development
- Registration platform replacement
- New registration/payment functionality
- Major redesign after approval
- Additional templates or pages beyond the selected package
- Copywriting beyond the selected package
- New integrations
- Ad spend
- Legal, tax, privacy, accessibility, or race-operation advice
- Ongoing work outside the approved first-year package or separately approved follow-up scope

Out-of-scope work requires written approval and may be quoted separately.

## 7. Hosting and third-party services

StartLine Sites may use third-party services such as Netlify, Cloudflare, Supabase, Stripe, Google Analytics, Google Search Console, Resend, domain registrars, email providers, and registration platforms.

StartLine Sites is not responsible for outages, policy changes, pricing changes, data issues, account suspensions, search algorithm changes, or service interruptions caused by third-party providers.

The customer owns and remains responsible for its domain name, registrar account, race registration platform, payment processor, email account, race operations, and legal compliance.

## 8. Ownership and license

The customer owns:

- Its domain name
- Its race name, trademarks, and supplied brand assets
- Customer-supplied photos, logos, text, and race information
- Registration data and customer/runner data controlled by the customer or its registration platform
- Final customer-specific website content after full payment

StartLine Sites owns:

- Templates
- Reusable layouts and components
- Code libraries
- Build systems
- Internal tools
- Automations
- Deployment workflows
- Know-how
- Pre-existing materials
- Unused concepts and drafts

After full payment, the customer receives a license to use the final launched website for its race. If service ends and the account is paid in full, StartLine Sites will provide a static export or reasonable handoff of the final production site. The handoff does not include StartLine Sites' proprietary template engine, internal tools, automations, deployment systems, private credentials, or reusable source libraries unless separately agreed.

## 9. Portfolio and attribution

StartLine Sites may reference the completed project in its portfolio, case studies, marketing, screenshots, and social posts, including the customer's race name, logo, website link, and general results.

The customer may opt out in writing if confidentiality is required.

StartLine Sites may include a small footer credit unless the customer requests removal or the proposal says otherwise.

## 10. No guarantee of results

StartLine Sites will use commercially reasonable SEO, performance, accessibility, and conversion practices.

StartLine Sites does not guarantee search rankings, website traffic, registrations, revenue, sponsorships, race attendance, ad performance, email deliverability, or any specific business result. Outcomes depend on many factors outside StartLine Sites' control, including race reputation, pricing, location, competition, marketing budget, weather, registration platform, third-party algorithms, and customer operations.

## 11. Suspension and nonpayment

StartLine Sites may pause work, launch, support, hosting, maintenance, reporting, or handoff if invoices are overdue, payment fails, required information is missing, or customer actions create a security, legal, or operational risk.

Suspension does not waive amounts due.

## 12. Cancellation and termination

Either party may terminate for material breach if the breach is not cured within [10 / 30] days after written notice.

StartLine Sites may terminate or suspend service immediately for nonpayment, unlawful content, abusive conduct, security risk, infringement claims, third-party terms violations, or actions that create unacceptable operational risk.

If the customer cancels before launch, the customer is responsible for work completed and approved expenses through the cancellation date. The deposit is applied against work performed and is not refundable once work has begun.

If the customer cancels after launch, any approved ongoing service follows the written terms for that service; otherwise no recurring service is assumed. Fees already billed are non-refundable except where required by law or agreed in writing.

After termination, StartLine Sites may stop hosting and maintenance unless a separate transition arrangement is agreed. The customer is responsible for DNS changes and third-party account continuity.

## 13. Warranties and corrections

For [30] days after launch, StartLine Sites will correct material defects where the delivered site does not match the approved proposal and the issue is within StartLine Sites' control.

This correction obligation does not cover customer-supplied errors, customer changes, third-party outages, registration platform issues, domain/DNS issues, search engine behavior, or new requests outside the approved scope.

## 14. Limitation of liability

To the fullest extent allowed by law, StartLine Sites is not liable for lost profits, lost registrations, lost revenue, lost data, business interruption, indirect damages, consequential damages, third-party outages, search engine changes, registrar issues, payment processor issues, registration platform issues, customer-supplied content claims, or race-operation issues.

StartLine Sites' total liability is limited to the amount the customer paid to StartLine Sites for the affected setup project or, for separately approved recurring-service claims, the amount paid for that service in the three months before the claim.

Some jurisdictions do not allow certain limitations, so this section applies only to the extent permitted by law.

## 15. Customer content and legal compliance

The customer is responsible for the accuracy, legality, and rights clearance of all race information, images, maps, sponsor materials, pricing, policies, disclaimers, accessibility statements, privacy notices, and registration details.

StartLine Sites may refuse or remove content that appears unlawful, infringing, misleading, unsafe, or inconsistent with third-party platform rules.

## 16. Independent contractor

StartLine Sites is an independent contractor, not an employee, partner, agent, joint venturer, or race operator.
